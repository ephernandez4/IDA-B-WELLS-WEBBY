Black History Month - Ida B Wells and her Legacy

Early Life

African American journalist, abolitionist, and feminist Ida B. Wells. Wells was the eldest child of James and Lizzie Wells and was born an enslaved person in Holly Springs, Mississippi, on July 16, 1862. In addition to other causes, she worked for women's suffrage. She also led a campaign against lynchings.

Career

Ida B. Wells was a Journalist and Publisher.
